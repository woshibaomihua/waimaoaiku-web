What shoppers are really looking for

Most air purifier searches are not just product searches. They are problem searches. A visitor may be trying to reduce pollen discomfort, manage pet dander, control smoke odor, choose a bedroom unit, compare CADR, or understand replacement filter cost. A high-converting page should answer that exact intent before pushing a product.

How to evaluate the right model

  • Room size: Match the purifier to the actual room and desired air changes per hour.
  • CADR: Compare public CADR or final lab-tested CADR rather than vague coverage claims.
  • Filter system: HEPA-style particle filtration and activated carbon solve different problems.
  • Noise: Bedroom and nursery buyers need quiet operation information.
  • Filter replacement: Maintenance cost and availability are part of the buying decision.
